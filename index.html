Mawasiliano Centre for Public Policy (MCPP)

Mawasiliano Centre for Public Policy (MCPP) is a non-profit organization committed to harnessing and advancing strategic communication to foster democracy, good governance, sustainable development, and human dignity. Our work is grounded in the conviction that transformative social change is driven by intentional, inclusive, and people-centred communication.

We collaborate with communities, researchers, civil society, political actors, policymakers and citizens to shape public discourse that serves the public interest, with a particular focus on African contexts. Guided by ethics, transparency, and respect for human agency, we prioritize contextually sensitive messaging and audience engagement.

In today’s complex media landscape, we employ integrated, multi-channel strategies informed by theoretical rigor, empirical research, and evidence-based practices to drive impactful societal transformation.

We invite you to join us in building a more democratic and inclusive future through the power of strategic communication.

Our Vision

A democratic and inclusive society transformed through ethical, strategic communication that advances good governance, sustainable development, and human dignity.

Our Mission

To harness and advance strategic communication as a catalyst to advance democracy, good governance, sustainable development, and human dignity.

What we do

To realize its mission and vision, the Mawasiliano Centre for Public Policy (MCPP) operates through six strategic programmes: Research and Policy Analysis, Advocacy for Communication Rights and Ethics, Training and Capacity Building, Promoting Media and Information Literacy, Enhancing Government and Public Sector Communication, and Driving Social Change through Communication.

These programmes leverage rigorous interdisciplinary research, evidence-based policy advocacy, impactful training and capacity building, inclusive civic engagement, and culturally responsive communicative interventions to advance democracy, good governance, sustainable development, and human dignity across Africa and beyond.

Research & Policy Analysis

We conduct interdisciplinary research and policy analysis to explore the role of strategic communication in advancing democracy, good governance, sustainable development, and human dignity, generating evidence-based insights to shape effective communication policies and practices.

Advocacy for communication, rights and ethics

We advocate for policies and practices that uphold freedom of expression, freedom of the media, access to information, and digital rights. We also advocate for ethical communication practices in governance, public discourse, media practices and civic engagement.

Training and Capacity Building

We provide targeted training and capacity building programmes to strengthen the strategic communication skills of government, public sector institutions, and key development sectors like health, education, environment, and human rights.

Promoting Media and Information Literacy (MIL)

Our programmes aim to counter disinformation and misinformation, strengthen democratic participation, promote peace, and build resilience to harmful narratives.

Enhancing Government & Public Sector Communication

We work to integrate strategic communication into government and public sector operations, improving service delivery, citizen engagement, and the achievement of national development goals.

Driving Social Change through Communication

We leverage strategic communication to address critical issues in health, population, education, environment, and human rights, fostering knowledge, positive attitudes, and behaviours for transformative societal outcomes.

Why Work with us?

We put people at the heart of policy. Our approach blends rigorous research with real-world insight, ensuring that every solution is informed, inclusive, and grounded in local context. Whether shaping strategy, facilitating dialogue, or guiding public initiatives, we deliver results that are thoughtful, impactful, and built on trust.
